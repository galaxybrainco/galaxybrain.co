Risograph Workshop Instructions

We’re excited to have you at our upcoming workshop! These instructions are meant to give you an impression of what the day’s going to be like, which should help you have a good time. This is not meant to be homework; We don’t believe in homework.

What we do believe in is what artist Corita Kent called “plork”. With plork, you get to have fun and feel accomplished when you’re done. Let’s get to plork!

What’s Going to Happen?

Here’s our entire agenda:

  1. Philip and Liam greet you and pour you a drink. Philip knows a lot about wine and drinks, and will have hard and soft beverages on hand.
  2. Liam shows you how the Risograph works, answers your questions, and shows you some cool prints made by us and others for inspiration and edification.
  3. You print an image you’ve brought, and/or you make something interesting to print. You experiment. We help out, advise, and pour.
  4. You go home with a couple dozen or so prints of your own. You feel great, hopefully.

What Can I Print?

A lot of things! Here are your constraints:

  • Our Riso prints on paper up to 8.5"x14", and most of our paper stock is 8.5"x11".
  • Our ink colors are Federal Blue, Bright Red, Green, Yellow, and Black. You can mix them on a single print for lots of tones. Here’s a handy reference on Risograph ink colors.
  • Due to the way Risography works, you need to separate each color into a layer on its own so it can receive a separate ink pass. If you’ve ever done screen printing, this will be familiar.

Small art prints will work great, and if you’re ambitious you can get away with making a single-sheet folded zine.

Given the time constraint, you’ll have the most success with two or three colors. If you’ve got a cool idea that requires more, let’s talk about it. We’re here to help.

What’s a Risograph, Exactly?

The pithy answer is, it’s a humble duplicator (like a photocopier) that can be tricked into becoming an amazing art tool.

A more useful answer is, it’s an automated screen printer that uses unique stencil and ink techniques to create handmade-looking prints automatically.

For a truly accurate answer, check out Risology Club’s explanation.
